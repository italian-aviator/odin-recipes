Pasta alla Carbonara

Back home

Description

Pasta alla carbonara is a typical italian dish that originated in the city of Rome. It is simply pasta with a sauce that is made from raw eggs, pecorino cheese, pepper and guanciale, the pig's cheek. There's still a lot of arguments regarding what type of pasta to use, but don't worry, I won't be talking about that here. The dish is fairly heavy and not raccomended for someone on a diet. Although the recipe is fairly simple, it's easy to mess up by cooking the egg and producing a type of frittata... not the best experince.

Ingredients

Ingredients are roughly calculated for 4 people, quantity may vary based on how angy you are.

  • Pasta 600g
  • Eggs 6
  • Guanciale 300g
  • pecorino cheese as needed (about 80/100g)
  • pepper as needed

Remember that Pasta alla carbonaraisn't meant to be difficult so don't stress too much about the quantity. Do it as you like it, that's what matter.

Steps

  1. Cut the guanciale, if this is already done skip this step.
  2. Put the guanciale in a big enough pan and start cooking it until it's crispy. Do not add any oil, its fat will be sufficient.
  3. Start cooking the pasta (For those of you who don't know how to do it: boil the water, add some salt, put the pasta in for the amount of time reported in the package).
  4. Separate the eggs' yolk from the egg white.
  5. Add to the pecorino cheese and the pepper to the yolk.
  6. Mix until the sauce becomes dense and difficult to mix, be careful not to form any lump.
  7. Add the liquid fat from the guanciale to the sauce. Attention: the fat may be hot and might cook the egg, for this reason add it slowly and while mixing.
  8. Optional: add more pecorino cheese and pepper.
  9. Add some cooking water to the sauce to make it more fluid shortly before the pasta is done cooking.
  10. Drain the pasta and add it in the pan with the guanciale.
  11. Turn on the heat at a low intensity.
  12. Start adding the sauce while moving the pan on and off the heat and while mixing it all.
  13. Switch off the heat and serve the pasta after a shower of pecorino cheese.
  14. Enjoy!
